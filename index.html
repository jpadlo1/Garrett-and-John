Activities & Walks

San Francisco is best explored on foot. Below are our favorite neighborhood walks, parks, and day trips to make the most of your time in the city.

Saturday Mornings
Ferry Building Farmer's Market

One of the country's great farmers markets, right on the Embarcadero. Go Saturday morning for a visit to both the outdoor market and the marketplace inside. Our personal favorite: Primavera Mexican, tucked in the back of the building for breakfast (cash only). The strawberries from Yerena Farms are not to be missed either — buy more than you think you need.

What to Get
  • Yerena Farm's Strawberries — The best in the state. Buy a flat.
  • Primavera Mexican — Our favorite. Find them in the back of the building. Cash only. Worth the line.
  • Hog Island Oyster Bar — Fresh Tomales Bay oysters on the water.
  • Blue Bottle Coffee — Right inside the building.
Our Favorite Walk
Our Favorite Way to See the Bridge
Marina Green & Crissy Field to Fort Point
Marina District to Presidio · ~2.5 miles one way

This is our favorite walk in the city — and in our opinion, the single best way to experience the Golden Gate Bridge. You'll start at the Philz Coffee truck parked at Marina Green, then follow the water's edge through Crissy Field all the way to Fort Point, the Civil War-era fortress tucked directly under the southern anchorage of the bridge. The views get more spectacular with every step. Grab an Uber back when you're done.

The Walk
  • Start: Philz Coffee truck at Marina Green — get your coffee, then hit the path heading west along the water.
  • Marina Green: Wide, flat waterfront lawn with sweeping views of the bay, Sausalito, and the bridge in the distance. Walk west along the path.
  • Crissy Field: A restored tidal marsh and beach — one of the most beautiful spots in SF. The bridge gets closer with every step.
  • Fort Point: A Civil War fortress right under the bridge's southern tower. Walk underneath the arch and look straight up — the view is extraordinary.
  • Return: Grab an Uber back when you're done. Walk takes 45–60 minutes at a relaxed pace.

Parks & Outdoors
Park Highlight: Golden Gate Park
A San Francisco Essential
Golden Gate Park
Inner Sunset · 1,017 acres

Get an herbal latte at California Kahve and stroll through some highlights of the park.

Highlights
  • California Kahve: Start here. Herbal lattes and excellent coffee near the park's east entrance.
  • Carousel: The 1912 carousel just inside the park — a charming, old-fashioned detour.
  • AIDS Memorial Grove: A quiet, beautifully maintained grove in the heart of the park. Worth a moment.
  • California Academy of Sciences: World-class natural history museum with a living roof. Worth a visit if you have time.
  • SF Botanical Garden: 55 acres of plants from around the world. Peaceful and beautiful.

Day Trip: Sausalito
Further Afield · Marin County
Cross the Golden
Gate Bridge

A perfect half-day trip: walk or rent a bike to cross the Golden Gate Bridge, then coast down into the waterfront village of Sausalito for lunch or dinner. The ferry back to the Ferry Building is a beautiful way to end it.

  • Joinery — Dockside fast casual on the waterfront. Order at the counter, eat outside. Excellent.
  • Fish — Another dockside spot on the water. Known for their fresh-catch sandwiches and chowder.
  • Ferry back — Take the Golden Gate Ferry from Sausalito back to the Ferry Building. Views of the city skyline on the way home.
